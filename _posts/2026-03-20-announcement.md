We are writing to inform the TEN community pursuant to Article 88 of Regulation (EU) 2023/1114, that Obscuro Limited ("Obscuro Labs"), the UK-based software company that developed the TEN Protocol intellectual property and who was contracted by the TEN Network Association to provide engineering, operational and marketing services for the TEN Protocol, is undergoing a restructuring process in the United Kingdom. The TEN Network Association Board made the decision to terminate the service agreement with Obscuro Limited on the basis it could no longer provide the required services.

## What this means for TEN Protocol

The TEN Network Association is a separate, independent legal entity incorporated in Switzerland. The Association's purpose is to govern the TEN Protocol network and steward its long-term development in the interests of the community.

The core engineering work on TEN Protocol has been completed. However, Obscuro Limited is the owner of the TEN Protocol intellectual property. The Association remains committed to exploring all available paths to progress the protocol.

## Next steps

The Association's immediate priorities are:

1. Completing a full assessment of the current state of the protocol, infrastructure and any ongoing obligations
2. Evaluating operational contingencies, including identifying alternative operators
3. Maintaining active engagement with relevant stakeholders

We understand this news is disappointing, particularly for those who have supported the TEN Protocol from the early days. Development is code complete and security audits have been completed. We will share updates as they become available.
